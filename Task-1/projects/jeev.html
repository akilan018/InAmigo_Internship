About This Project

Why Project Jeev?

India's streets are home to millions of stray animals — dogs, cows, cats, and birds — who survive in harsh conditions without food, medical care, or protection. Project Jeev was born from the conviction that compassion has no species boundary.

Our dedicated volunteers hit the streets daily — rain or shine — to feed strays, rescue injured animals, connect them with veterinary care, and raise community awareness about responsible animal treatment. We also campaign against animal cruelty and work to promote adoption over abandonment.

As our LinkedIn posts reflect: "Saving the voiceless is not just kindness — it is responsibility." Every meal given, every wound treated, and every life saved is a testament to human compassion at its finest.

Our Objectives

Goals of Project Jeev

Six commitments to protect and care for India's voiceless creatures.

01🍲

Daily Feeding Drives

Ensure stray dogs, cows, and other animals in urban and semi-urban areas receive nutritious food every day through organized volunteer feeding routes.

02🚑

Emergency Animal Rescue

Operate a rapid response network for injured, sick, or trapped animals — coordinating with local veterinarians and animal shelters for immediate medical intervention.

03💉

Vaccination & Sterilization

Partner with vets to provide free vaccination and sterilization drives for stray dogs — controlling population humanely while preventing diseases like rabies.

04🏠

Adoption Promotion

Campaign for adopting stray animals as pets instead of buying from breeders — reducing the stray population and giving deserving animals loving forever homes.

05📢

Anti-Cruelty Campaigns

Raise public awareness about animal cruelty laws (PCA Act, 1960), encourage reporting of animal abuse, and sensitize communities to treat animals with dignity.

06🌡️

Summer & Winter Care

Set up water bowls for animals during scorching summers and provide warmth and shelter resources during cold winters — ensuring year-round compassionate care.

What We Do

Our Activities

Daily acts of compassion that save, protect, and heal.

01

Daily Street Feeding Routes

Trained volunteers cover designated feeding routes each morning, providing food and clean water to stray dogs and cows in urban neighborhoods, markets, and industrial areas.

02

Emergency Rescue & First Aid

Rapid response to animal injury reports — volunteers provide immediate first aid, safe transport to veterinary facilities, and follow-up care for recovering animals.

03

Vaccination Camps

Free anti-rabies and distemper vaccination camps for stray dogs, organized in partnership with local veterinarians and animal welfare organizations.

04

Animal Welfare Awareness Sessions

Community sessions in schools and housing societies that teach responsible pet ownership, animal rights, and the importance of reporting cruelty — building a compassionate generation.

05

Summer Water & Shade Drives

Installation of water bowls and shade shelters for street animals during peak summer months — preventing heat stroke and dehydration in the scorching Indian heat.
