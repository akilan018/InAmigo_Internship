About This Project

Why Project Seva?

Every day in India, millions of people go to bed hungry. They sleep on streets, work in the scorching heat without a meal, and survive without basic necessities. Project Seva exists because no one should suffer in silence while others have the power to help.

Our volunteers serve meals on the streets of Indian cities, organize mass food distribution during festivals and extreme weather, run clothing donation drives for winter relief, and conduct blood donation camps that have saved hundreds of lives. As we say: "Service doesn't wait for comfort — it shows up where it's needed most."

Seva is also about dignity in giving. We don't just hand out packets — we sit with people, we listen to their stories, we see their humanity. That's what separates service from charity.

Our Objectives

Goals of Project Seva

Multi-dimensional service addressing hunger, health, and human dignity.

01🍲

Zero Hunger Initiative

Reach as many hungry individuals on the streets as possible — providing cooked meals, dry ration packets, and nutritional snacks to laborers, homeless persons, and families in need.

02🩸

Blood Donation Camps

Organize regular blood donation camps in colleges, offices, and public spaces — saving lives by building a ready blood supply for hospitals, accident victims, and surgery patients.

03👕

Clothing Distribution

Collect and distribute winter clothes, blankets, and essential garments to homeless individuals, migrant workers, and flood/disaster-affected families across India.

04🎁

Festival Drives

Special distributions during Diwali, Eid, Christmas, and other festivals ensuring that every person — regardless of economic status — experiences the joy and warmth of celebration.

05🌡️

Extreme Weather Relief

Deploy relief teams during summer heat waves, monsoon floods, and winter cold waves — providing ORS packets, raincoats, umbrellas, and warm meals to vulnerable communities.

06🏥

Healthcare Awareness

Conduct basic health check-up camps in partnership with medical volunteers — providing free BP, sugar, and weight screening to underprivileged communities who lack access to healthcare.

What We Do

Our Activities

Compassionate actions that feed, clothe, and heal communities.

01

Daily Street Food Service

Volunteers prepare or collect cooked food and distribute it to laborers, rickshaw pullers, homeless persons, and street children on designated routes across the city, every single day.

02

Mass Blood Donation Camps

Organized camps at colleges, corporates, and public events — with pre-registration, healthy snacks, and certificates — making blood donation accessible, safe, and rewarding for donors.

03

Winter Blanket & Clothing Drives

Collection drives where the community donates old (clean) warm clothes and blankets, which are then sorted, washed, and distributed to homeless individuals during the winter months.

04

Festival Celebrations for the Underprivileged

Special events where children on the streets receive sweets, gifts, and a warm meal — ensuring they experience the joy of festivals that the rest of us often take for granted.

05

Emergency Ration Kit Distribution

During floods, droughts, or COVID-like emergencies, rapid deployment of ration kits (rice, dal, oil, spices) to affected families — providing food security during the most difficult times.
