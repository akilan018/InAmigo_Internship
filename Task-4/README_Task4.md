# 🎨 NGO Website Feature Highlights
### InAmigos Foundation — Task 4: Figma Design Task

---

## 📌 About This Project

This project was completed as **Task 4** of the **InAmigos Foundation** internship programme.

The objective was to identify key features that an NGO website should have and present them visually as a design layout — similar to a Figma prototype — with labels and notes explaining each feature's purpose and importance.

> **Tool Used:** HTML + CSS (Figma-equivalent design, exported to PDF)
> **Output:** 11-page visual design document

## 🎯 Features Designed (6 Key Sections)

### 01 · 🏠 Hero Banner + Donation Section
The homepage first impression. Includes:
- Full navigation bar with a highlighted **Donate Now** CTA
- Bold mission headline with subtext
- **Embedded donation widget** with preset amounts (₹200 / ₹500 / ₹1000 / ₹2000 / ₹5000 / Custom)
- UPI / Card / Net Banking input fields
- 80G tax exemption + SSL trust badges
- Impact statistics bar (volunteers, funds raised, communities, states)

**Why it matters:** The hero is the first thing a visitor sees. An embedded donation card removes all friction — the visitor doesn't need to navigate anywhere to give.

---

### 02 · 📊 Impact Showcase
Proof that the NGO's work is real and measurable. Includes:
- 6 color-coded impact cards — Education, Health, Environment, Employment, Food, Volunteers
- Real numbers: 8,200 students · 3,540 health camps · 42,000 trees · 12,400 volunteers
- Dual-column **annual goals progress bars** with live percentage fills
- Color-coded by cause area (teal, coral, gold, green, purple, navy)

**Why it matters:** Donors and volunteers want proof before they commit. Transparent impact numbers build trust and drive recurring support.

---

### 03 · 🙋 Volunteer Registration Form
A frictionless signup experience. Includes:
- Name, email, phone, city fields
- Area of interest dropdown
- **Skill tag selector** (Teaching, Web Design, Photography, Content Writing, etc.)
- Privacy statement and security badge
- Companion panel with 6 reasons why a good form matters

**Why it matters:** A well-designed form collects the right data to match volunteers to the right cause — reducing coordinator workload and volunteer drop-off.

---

### 04 · 💬 Testimonials & Stories
Social proof from real voices. Includes:
- 3 testimonial cards — Student Volunteer, Corporate CSR Partner, Monthly Donor
- Star ratings and italic quote styling
- **Featured Story block** — beneficiary turned entrepreneur (dark navy banner)
- Avatar initials with gradient color coding

**Why it matters:** Authentic stories are the most powerful driver of first-time donations and volunteer sign-ups. People trust people, not organizations.

---

### 05 · 🎯 Active Campaigns
Goal-based fundraising with urgency. Includes:
- 3 campaign cards — Digital Classrooms, Rural Health Camps, Tree Plantation Drive
- Status tags: **ACTIVE** · **URGENT** · **ONGOING**
- Real-time progress bars with funds raised vs goal
- Days remaining + donor count
- Insight block: *"Campaign pages raise 3× more than generic donate buttons"*

**Why it matters:** Specific goals + visible progress bars tap into completion psychology. Donors rally around causes, not abstract giving pages.

---

### 06 · ✉ Newsletter Signup + Contact
Retaining supporters between campaigns. Includes:
- Newsletter form with **interest tag checkboxes** (Education, Volunteering, Health, Campaigns)
- Subscriber count (8,400+) and trust copy
- 3 contact cards — Email, Phone, Office Address
- Unsubscribe assurance copy

**Why it matters:** Email capture turns one-time visitors into long-term community members. Segmented newsletters keep different supporter types engaged with relevant content.

## 💡 Recommendations for InAmigos Foundation

Based on this design research, here are the top 3 features to implement first on the InAmigos website:

1. **Donation Widget on Homepage** — Highest ROI. Even a simple embed with 3–4 preset amounts will significantly increase online giving.
2. **Impact Numbers Section** — Easy to implement, massive trust builder. Update quarterly.
3. **Volunteer Form with Skill Tags** — Replace generic contact forms with a structured signup that captures useful coordinator data.
